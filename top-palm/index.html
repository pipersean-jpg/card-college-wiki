    The Top Palm¶

    Sources: CC2 Ch. 18 (pp. 273–292)

    What This Is¶

    The Top Palm is the act of secretly removing one or more cards from the top of the deck into the right hand's palm. It is the most-used palm in card magic — whenever the phrase "palm the top card" appears in a description, it means this technique.

    Basic Technique¶

    The deck is in left-hand dealing position. The right hand approaches from above to "square" the deck:

    1. The right hand settles over the top of the deck, fingers at the outer end, thumb at the inner end
    2. The left thumb pushes the top card(s) slightly to the right — about half an inch
    3. As the right hand contacts the deck in the squaring action, the right fingers press the pushed-over card into the palm
    4. The right hand lifts away, apparently squaring the top of the deck, while actually leaving with the palmed card

    The push-over by the left thumb is the key. It moves the card into the path of the right palm without any visible action from the right hand. The right hand does nothing suspicious — it merely presses down and lifts away in the most natural squaring motion.

    Palming Multiple Cards from the Top¶

    The same technique applies to two, three, or more cards. The left thumb executes a double push-over or triple push-over (taught in CC1 Ch. 14 as the Single, Double and Multiple Push-over). Multiple cards are pushed as a unit, then palmed together.

    This is used for ace productions, packet additions, and multiple card transfers.

    Concealing the Palmed Cards in the Hand (CC2 Ch. 18)¶

    Giobbi devotes an entire section to this — not just the palm itself, but what to do with the palmed cards during the moments between palming and replacing:

    • Gesturing: The palming hand can point, gesture toward the spectator, or tap the table without exposing the card, as long as the back of the hand faces the audience
    • Putting the hand in the pocket: The natural action of reaching into a pocket — "searching for something" — provides cover and can be used to introduce a preparation (such as a gimmick) or to secretly place the palmed card elsewhere
    • Resting the hand on the table: A hand resting palm-down on the table looks natural and conceals the card beneath it

    Replacing Palmed Cards¶

    The reverse action: returning the palmed card(s) to the top of the deck invisibly. The most common method:

    The right hand approaches the deck from above — as if to square it again. The palmed card lies under the right fingers. As the hand settles on top, it rides the palmed card back onto the top of the deck, and the squaring action squares everything flush. From the front, it looks identical to any other squaring.

    Giobbi includes subtleties: the right hand should approach slightly tilted so the card's edge doesn't catch the near edge of the deck; the motion should be confident and unhurried.

    Use in Practice¶

    The top palm is the standard method for: - Apparently losing a card in the deck after a force, then producing it from an impossible location - Loading a card into a different location (pocket, wallet, under an object) - Switching out a card before dealing it to the spectator - Setting up a "card to pocket" effect
