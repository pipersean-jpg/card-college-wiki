# Juan Tamariz

**Full name:** Juan Tamariz Martel (born 1942)  
**Nationality:** Spanish  
**Based in:** Madrid

---

## Who He Is

Juan Tamariz is one of the greatest close-up magicians of the modern era and arguably the most creative card magician alive. He is a member of the Escuela Mágica de Madrid along with Arturo de Ascanio, and his approach to magic — playful, theatrical, deeply psychological — has been enormously influential worldwide.

Giobbi's connection to Tamariz is celebrated explicitly: in the dedication of CC1, Giobbi calls Tamariz "my role model as an artist and a human being." Tamariz wrote the foreword to *Card College Volume 1* — an enthusiastic endorsement that positioned the book immediately as a serious work. Tamariz writes in the foreword that Giobbi "had to be someone with the deepest knowledge of the literature of card magic — in English, German, French, Italian and Spanish."

---

## Contributions Attributed to Tamariz in Giobbi's Series

**The Secret Addition / Gaze Technique** (CC1 Ch. 14): Giobbi explicitly credits Tamariz with the *dynamic use of the gaze* in the secret addition of cards — a specific technique for managing eye contact and visual attention during the loading of extra cards beneath a displayed packet. This contribution demonstrates Tamariz's philosophical approach: the technique is not a hand action but a *perceptual* action.

Tamariz is also the author of *Sonata* (Editorial Frakson, Madrid, 1991), cited in Giobbi's recommended reading list in CC2 — "In this book you will find many of the routines of one of the greatest close-up magicians of modern times."

---

## Tamariz's Theoretical Framework: The "Magical Trance"

Tamariz has written extensively about the psychology of magic. His most important contribution to theory — the idea of leading the spectator into what he calls a "magical trance" — describes the state where spectators stop analytically searching for explanations and surrender completely to the magical experience.

This aligns closely with Giobbi's own description of the ultimate goal of presentation: the feeling of "boundless astonishment" where spectators "simply believe you and give themselves over to the experience."

---

## Tamariz's Performance Style

Tamariz is famous for a highly interactive, theatrical, sometimes clownish performing style that is the antithesis of the cool, precise style associated with Vernon. Where Vernon advocated restraint and naturalness, Tamariz embraces expressiveness, humor, and theatrical exaggeration.

Both approaches serve the same ultimate goal — creating genuine wonder — through different paths. Giobbi presents both without privileging one, understanding that each approach is suited to different personalities and contexts.

---

## The Foreword to Card College Volume 1

Tamariz's foreword is generous and specific. He identifies what makes Card College unique: it combines technical depth with theoretical rigor and practical performance wisdom in a way no previous book had achieved in English. He compares it favorably to the books of Jean Hugard and Frederick Braue, which had served as the standard English-language card magic course for a previous generation.
