# Bottom Palm and Gambler's Cop

**Sources:** CC3 Ch. 36 (pp. 713–730)

---

## The Left-hand Bottom Palm

Palming a card from the bottom of the deck into the left hand.

The deck is held in left-hand dealing position. The right hand approaches to take the deck from above (for a cut or a shuffle). As the right hand approaches, the left hand's fingers curl upward, and the bottom card slides into the left palm — pinned between the third and fourth fingers at its right side, and at the heel of the hand at its left side. The right hand takes the deck away; the left hand is left holding the palmed card, apparently empty.

The bottom palm requires excellent finger control to execute quietly. The card must not snap or scrape against the bottom of the deck as it is taken, and the left hand must not change shape noticeably as the card is loaded.

---

## The Right-hand Bottom Palm

Palming a card from the bottom into the right hand.

The deck is in left-hand dealing position. The right hand takes the deck from below by the ends, and in the act of lifting, the bottom card is pinned into the right palm. The remaining deck continues upward while the palmed card stays in the palm.

Used in gambling demonstrations and in tricks requiring a card to appear or disappear from the bottom of the deck.

---

## The Right-hand Bottom Palm of Multiple Cards

The same technique applied to a small packet at the bottom. Used to secretly remove several setup cards from the bottom while apparently transferring the deck.

---

## The Gambler's Cop

A palm variant where the card is held at the **inner end** of the right hand — between the heel of the palm and the curled little finger. The card is not flat in the palm but protrudes slightly past the inner wrist.

The Gambler's Cop is associated with card cheating technique — specifically the "cooler" switch (introducing a stacked deck). It offers different angle coverage than the standard top palm: spectators directly in front of the performer are better fooled by the cop than by a flat palm.

### Management Strategies for the Gambler's Cop (CC3 Ch. 36)

Giobbi discusses how to position the body and hands to manage the angles while holding a card in the cop. The primary strategy: keep the inner end of the right hand angled down and toward the body, minimizing the protrusion visible from any angle.

### Replacing Cards in the Gambler's Cop Under the Deck

A specific technique for invisibly returning a copped card to the bottom of the deck. The right hand appears to place the deck on the table normally — but the copped card is left behind on the bottom as the hand sets the deck down.

---

## Replacing Palmed Cards on the Bottom of the Deck (CC3 Ch. 36)

Returning a card to the *bottom* of the deck (rather than the top) is sometimes required. Giobbi teaches a technique where the palmed card is added to the bottom during the action of placing the deck down or during a cutting motion.

---

## Norman Houghton's Pocket Extraction (CC3 Ch. 36)

**Attributed to:** Norman Houghton

A complete production sequence: a card is palmed, then "found" in the inner jacket pocket — created by the performer reaching into the pocket and leaving the palmed card behind, then immediately "finding" it there. Giobbi teaches two versions depending on which pocket is used.

---

## Producing a Card from the Jacket Pocket

Two variants (CC3 Ch. 36):
- **Version 1:** Card is loaded into the inner right jacket pocket and produced by a natural reach
- **Version 2:** Card is loaded via a slightly different angle, for performers using a different pocket position
