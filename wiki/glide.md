# The Glide

**Sources:** CC1 Ch. 8 (pp. 119–126)

---

## What the Glide Is

The **Glide** is a simple and elegant sleight performed with the deck held face down in the left hand. When the performer appears to deal the bottom card, the left middle finger secretly pulls the bottom card back a fraction of an inch — and the card that is actually dealt is the *second card from the bottom*, not the bottom card.

The effect: the performer appears to deal from a known position (the face of the deck), but is actually dealing a different card from what was shown.

---

## How It Works

The deck is held face down in the left hand with the cards angled toward the floor — the standard glide position:

- The left thumb is at the upper left edge
- The left fingers curl around the lower edge, with the middle finger resting lightly on the back of the face card
- The deck is tipped at a slight angle so the face card's face can be shown to the audience

To execute the glide:

1. The face card is shown to the audience ("This is the three of clubs")
2. The left middle finger curls inward, drawing the face card back about half an inch while the hand returns to the horizontal position
3. The right thumb reaches under the deck and takes the now-exposed second card from the bottom, dealing it face down

To the audience, the bottom card (the named card) was dealt. In reality, it remains in the hand and a different card was dealt.

To end the glide and deal the actual face card, the middle finger pushes the bottom card back into alignment and it is dealt normally.

---

## Applications

**Deals and misdirections:** The glide allows the performer to appear to deal a specific card while actually dealing a neutral card, or vice versa.

**The Under-the-spread Force:** Related technique from CC1 Ch. 13 — the glide's principle is applied during a spread to force a specific card.

**The Glide Change:** The glide principle is also used in the card-switch context (CC4 Ch. 40) — see [Card Switches](card-switches.md).

---

## Classic Tricks Using the Glide (CC1 Ch. 8)

- **The Acrobat Family:** A three-of-a-kind plot where cards appear and disappear using the glide as the mechanism
- **The Coue Test:** A presentation built around suggestion and "autosuggestion" theory, using the glide as the hidden engine

These tricks appear in CC1 as illustrations of the glide's utility in performance.

---

## Notes on Naturalness

The glide is performed with the deck held at an unusual angle (tilted toward the performer). Giobbi addresses this: the angled position must be introduced naturally and maintained consistently so it does not look like a suspicious "trick position." The best way is to show the face of the deck as part of the presentation — the angle of showing the face is the same angle needed for the glide.
