# The Spread Cull

**Sources:** CC1 Ch. 13 (pp. 185–193)

---

## What Culling Is

**Culling** is the act of secretly gathering specific cards from the deck during an apparently normal spread or shuffle. The performer spreads through the cards as if simply showing them, and meanwhile moves specific cards (by suit, value, or identity) to a position from which they can be controlled or palmed.

The spread cull is the fundamental culling technique — the foundation from which more advanced methods are built.

---

## The Spread Cull (CC1 Ch. 13)

As cards are spread from the left hand into the right in a hand-to-hand spread, the left thumb can pull individual cards downward into the heel of the left palm rather than allowing them to pass into the right hand's packet. These "culled" cards build up under the left hand's growing packet.

The culling action uses the natural motion of the left thumb during the spread. Every card appears to pass from left to right; but certain cards silently divert downward and accumulate in the left palm. When the spread is complete, the culled cards can be transferred to the top or bottom of the deck.

### The Under-the-spread Force

A related technique: during a spread, a specific card is deliberately kept back (not allowed to pass) so that when the spectator stops the spread and takes a card, the only card available at the stop point is the force card. This is a force disguised as a free selection from a spread.

---

## What Makes Culling Difficult

Two things make the spread cull challenging:

1. **Maintaining an even, natural spread** while the left thumb is doing extra work — the spread must look smooth even as cards are being diverted
2. **Keeping the audience's eyes on the faces of the cards**, not on the left thumb's action. Giobbi notes that having a strong reason to show the cards (looking for a specific card, showing the faces to the audience) provides natural justification and keeps attention on the right place

---

## Tricks Using the Spread Cull (CC1 Ch. 13)

- **The Magic Phone Number:** A card is apparently randomly selected by dialing digits on a telephone pad — really a culling sequence
- **As You Like It:** An impromptu trick using culling to set up a small packet
