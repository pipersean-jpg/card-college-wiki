# Advanced Culling

**Sources:** CC4 Ch. 44 (pp. 895–910)

---

## Overview

Advanced culling builds on the spread cull (CC1 Ch. 13) to provide multiple methods for gathering specific cards during different kinds of handling. Where the basic spread cull uses the hand-to-hand spread, the advanced methods use table spreads, shuffle actions, and angle-based geometry.

---

## The Grismer Cull

**Attributed to:** Grismer

A cull performed during a riffle shuffle or shuffle-like action. The cards being gathered are separated out during the shuffle rather than during a spread. This is efficient because the cull and the shuffle are combined — no extra handling is needed.

---

## The Lessinout Shuffle

A specific shuffle-cull technique where cards are "lessened out" — reduced from the main packet — during an apparently normal overhand shuffle. Used to gather specific cards covertly while appearing to simply shuffle.

---

## The Downjog Cull

Cards are downjogged (pushed toward the performer) during a hand-to-hand spread. The jogged cards are gathered as a secret packet at the inner end. When the spread is closed, the downjogged cards can be taken out from the inner end and positioned as needed.

This is particularly effective because the downjogging action looks like a natural adjustment as the hands bring cards into alignment.

---

## The Above-the-spread Cull

A cull performed while cards are spread on the table (ribbon spread). As the performer's hand moves above the spread apparently examining or pointing to cards, specific cards are secretly lifted and transferred.

---

## The Green Angle Separation

**Attributed to:** Green

A cull that exploits the **angle** of the spread — by spreading cards at a specific diagonal angle, certain cards fall into a natural separation point. The performer then uses this separation to silently gather the required cards.

---

## Strategic Use of Culling

Giobbi discusses three tricks that use culling as their core mechanism (CC4 Ch. 44), noting that culling is most powerful when the reason for spreading the cards has a strong justification — such as checking that the deck is complete, showing that the cards are ordinary, or letting the spectator examine them. The more natural the reason to spread, the less attention falls on the cull.

---

## Culling vs. Stacking

**Culling** gathers cards from a random arrangement into a specific position in one session of handling.

**Stacking** builds a specific order of cards through shuffling, dealing, or setup.

The difference in performance: culling can be done in real time, from a shuffled deck, during the performance. Stacking typically requires setup before the trick begins.
