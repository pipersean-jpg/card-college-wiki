# The Side Steal and Diagonal Palm Shift

**Sources:** CC3 Ch. 38 (pp. 757–776)

---

## What the Side Steal Is

The **Side Steal** is a method for secretly removing a card from the center of the deck into the right palm, using a motion from the side of the deck. Unlike the top palm (which removes from the top) or the pass (which transposes halves), the side steal works silently from any position within the deck.

Its primary application: a card that has been inserted into the middle of the deck needs to be palmed — the side steal does this without any deck manipulation.

---

## The Side-steal Transfer

The basic action:

1. The selected card has been returned to the center of the deck; a break is held below it
2. The right hand grips the deck from above
3. The right thumb presses against the inner end of the selected card; the right fingers press against the outer end
4. The right hand slides the card sideways — out to the right — while the left hand's deck closes around the now-empty space
5. The card is palmed in the right hand

The "steal" happens from the side of the deck in a direction perpendicular to the deck's face. The key is that the left hand's closing action creates the impression that the deck is simply being squared.

---

## Covers for the Side Steal

The side steal requires cover for the moment the card exits the deck. The most practical covers:

- A squaring motion (the right hand squares the deck while stealing)
- Turning to face a different spectator (body rotation provides a moment of divided attention)
- A casual remark that makes the audience look up at the performer's face

---

## The Side-steal Bottom Placement and Variations

After stealing the card, it can be replaced to the bottom of the deck rather than palmed out completely. The card is transferred from the palm to the bottom during the natural replacement of the right hand on the deck.

---

## The Side-steal Palm

The complete palm: the card exits the deck entirely into the right palm and is held there. From here it can be produced, switched, or loaded elsewhere.

### The Simplified Side-steal Palm

A version that streamlines the angle of the steal — suited to performers who find the standard version mechanically awkward.

---

## The Diagonal Palm Shift

A related but distinct sleight for palming a card from the center of the deck. Rather than stealing from the side, the Diagonal Palm Shift moves the card **diagonally** — pushing one corner into the palm while the opposite corner remains in the deck momentarily.

The diagonal movement is more natural in some hand positions but requires the card to be at a specific position (usually near the top of the bottom half) for the geometry to work correctly.

---

## The Convincing Control Palm

Giobbi teaches this combination in CC3 Ch. 38: the Convincing Control (which controls the card to the top) is combined with a simultaneous palm. As the Convincing Control cut is made, the top card is palmed by the right hand. The result: the audience sees what appears to be a complete, clean cut, but the selected card has been removed from the deck entirely.

This combination is one of the strongest "card to pocket" setups in the series.
