    The Riffle Force¶

    Sources: CC1 Ch. 15 (pp. 224–226)

    What This Is¶

    The Riffle Force is a reliable mechanical force where the spectator "stops" the performer's riffle at any point, and the card at the stop position is the force card. Unlike the Classic Force (which relies on psychology and probability), the Riffle Force works through precise positioning — the force card is always in the correct location when the spectator says stop.

    The Riffle Force is often recommended as the first force a beginner should learn because it has an extremely high success rate and requires less psychological read of the spectator.

    How It Works¶

    1. The force card is on top of the deck
    2. The performer holds the deck in the right hand by the ends (end grip), face toward the performer, with the left thumb at the outer left corner
    3. The left thumb riffles the outer left corner of the deck, releasing cards upward one at a time while the spectator watches from the front
    4. The spectator says "stop" at any point
    5. Here is the key: the performer has been riffling from the bottom up. When the spectator says stop, the performer's thumb has released a portion of cards — but the force card is the original top card of the deck, which is the last card released
    6. The packet above the stopping point is lifted away, and the force card is on top of the remaining packet — the "card at the stop point"

    In one handling, the top card is always the card shown "at" the stop, regardless of where the spectator says stop. In another handling, the force card is positioned at a known depth and the performer controls where the riffle is when they feel the right moment. In either case, the spectator has the impression of completely free choice.

    Angle Management¶

    The deck must be angled so that the top card cannot be seen during the riffle. Giobbi notes this as the primary technical challenge: the face of the top (force) card must be hidden from spectators while the riffling of the rest of the deck is clearly visible.

    The solution: hold the deck so the outer corner riffles toward the spectators, and angle the face slightly away. Spectators see the action of the riffle — cards releasing — but cannot see the top card.

    The Riffle Force vs. The Classic Force¶

    Factor Classic Force Riffle Force
    Success rate ~80–90% for skilled performer Very close to 100%
    Suspicious appearance Can look exactly like a free selection Slightly more mechanical
    Requires psychological timing Yes No
    Requires setup position Top of deck Top of deck
    Usable from any angle Nearly yes No — angle restrictions

    Giobbi presents both in the same chapter, positioning them as complementary tools: the Classic Force for maximum natural appearance when conditions allow, the Riffle Force as the reliable backup.
